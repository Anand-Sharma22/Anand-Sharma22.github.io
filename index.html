About Me

I am Anand Sharma, a final-year BS–MS student in Physics at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Pune. I am currently a Visiting Student at the University of Luxembourg, working with Dr. Igor Poltavsky and Prof. Alexandre Tkatchenko on machine learning interatomic potentials and energy-landscape exploration.

My research is broadly at the interface of statistical physics and machine learning. I am drawn to questions about what machine learning models actually learn — what features matter, what the weights mean, and how to make predictions interpretable. In practice this has taken me from glassy dynamics to equivariant neural network force fields.

I have been fortunate to work closely with Dr. Misaki Ozawa and Prof. Daniele Coslovich (LIPhy CNRS / Grenoble, University of Trieste) over the past two years, spending time in Grenoble and collaborating remotely.

Alongside physics and ML, I spend time on badminton, table tennis, chess, and travelling. I like long conversations about the philosophy of science, and I am slowly learning to be a better reader.

Research

I work on problems where statistical physics and machine learning meet — interpretable models, feature selection, and understanding what structure tells us about dynamics. My current focus is benchmarking ML force fields without predefined test sets.
